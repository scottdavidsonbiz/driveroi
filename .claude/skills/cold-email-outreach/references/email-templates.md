# Cold Email Templates & Examples

## Template Structure

### Opening Email Template

```
Subject: [3-7 words, specific to their pain]

Hi [First Name],

[Personalized hook - company specific observation, recent event, or relevant context]

[Context: 2-3 sentences explaining the pain/challenge they likely face as a [persona] at a [segment] company]

[Your solution/angle: How you solve this specifically, what makes your approach different]

[Proof: Brief social proof, example, or credibility marker]

[CTA: Simple, clear ask - usually "Worth a quick call?" or similar]

[Your name]
```

## Bump Email Templates

### Bump 1: Additional Value Add

```
Subject: RE: [original subject]

Hi [Name],

Following up on my email from last week about [pain point].

Quick addition: Just published a breakdown of how [similar company] rebuilt their [relevant area] in 30 days. Thought you might find it useful even if you're not ready to chat.

[Link or offer to send]

Still worth 15 minutes to explore for [Company]?

[Your name]
```

### Bump 2: Simpler/More Direct

```
Subject: RE: [original subject]

Hi [Name],

I know you're busy - real quick question:

Is [pain point] something you're actively working on right now, or should I check back in a few months?

Either way is totally fine - just want to make sure I'm not filling your inbox unnecessarily.

[Your name]
```

### Bump 3: Different Angle

```
Subject: RE: [original subject]

Hi [Name],

Different angle on my email from last week:

[New pain point or perspective on the same challenge]

[Brief example or insight]

Worth a call, or should I step back?

[Your name]
```

## Email Length Guidelines

**Opening Email:**
- Minimum: 75 words
- Sweet spot: 100-125 words
- Maximum: 150 words

**Bump Email:**
- Minimum: 40 words
- Sweet spot: 60-80 words
- Maximum: 100 words

**Why these lengths:**
- Long enough to tell the full story
- Short enough to read in 30 seconds
- Forces you to be specific and focused

## Personalization Levels

### Level 1: Basic (Required)
- First name
- Company name
- Title/role

### Level 2: Solid (Recommended)
- Recent event (funding, hire, launch)
- Specific challenge for their segment
- Relevant proof point

### Level 3: Deep (High-value targets)
- Detailed company research
- Persona-specific pain observation
- Custom angle tailored to their situation
- Highly relevant social proof

## Tone Guidelines

**Do:**
- Professional but conversational
- Confident but not cocky
- Helpful but not pushy
- Specific and concrete

**Don't:**
- Overly formal ("I hope this correspondence finds you well...")
- Hype-filled ("revolutionary", "game-changing", "10x your...")
- Desperate ("Can I have 5 minutes of your time?")
- Generic ("We help companies grow faster...")

## Common Opening Lines That Work

1. **Recent event:** "I noticed [Company] just [raised Series B / hired VP Sales / launched new product]..."

2. **Peer reference:** "I was talking to [similar role] at [similar company] and they mentioned..."

3. **Observation:** "Saw your post on LinkedIn about [relevant topic]..."

4. **Pattern:** "Most [persona] at [segment] companies I talk to face..."

5. **Timing:** "Now that [Company] is [stage/milestone], you're probably dealing with..."

## Common Opening Lines to Avoid

1. "I hope this email finds you well" (generic, outdated)
2. "Can I have 5 minutes of your time?" (leads with ask)
3. "We're the leading provider of..." (about you, not them)
4. "I've been trying to reach you..." (desperate, pushy)
5. "I have a revolutionary solution..." (hype, unbelievable)

## CTA Variations

**Exploratory:**
- "Worth a quick call?"
- "15 minutes next week to explore?"
- "Would this be useful for [Company]?"

**Value-First:**
- "Want me to send over the framework?"
- "I can walk you through how [similar company] did this - interested?"
- "Happy to share the playbook - worth 15 minutes?"

**Permission-Based:**
- "Is this something you're actively working on?"
- "Wrong timing, or worth a conversation?"
- "Should I follow up in a few months, or explore now?"

**Direct:**
- "Let's chat?"
- "Quick call this week?"
- "15 minutes to discuss?"

## Formatting Best Practices

**Paragraphs:**
- 1-3 sentences per paragraph
- Blank line between paragraphs
- Use white space liberally

**Bold/Italics:**
- Sparingly, if at all
- Only to emphasize key point
- Never in CTA

**Links:**
- Only if adding value (case study, resource)
- Not to your homepage (unless they asked)
- Track clicks if possible

## Testing Strategy

Create 2-3 variations of each sequence:

**What to test:**
- Problem-first vs. solution-first
- Short (75 words) vs. medium (125 words)
- Different social proof
- Different CTAs
- Different subject lines

**Sample size:**
- Minimum 50 sends per variation
- 100+ for statistical significance

**What to measure:**
- Open rate (subject line test)
- Reply rate (email body test)
- Positive reply rate (quality test)
- Meeting booked rate (conversion test)
