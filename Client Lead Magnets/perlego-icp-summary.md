# Perlego ICP & Strategic Focus Analysis

**Prepared for:** Perlego B2B Sales Development
**Date:** January 13, 2026
**Purpose:** Define ICP for B2B expansion into higher education institutions and corporate L&D markets

---

## Company Overview

**Perlego** is a UK-based EdTech platform providing unlimited access to 1M+ academic books and textbooks from 9,000+ publishers including Cambridge University Press, Elsevier, and Wiley.

### Key Metrics (2024-2025):
- **Funding:** $75M total raised ($20M Series B in October 2024)
- **Content:** 1M+ titles from 9,000+ publishers
- **Partnerships:** 200+ university institutional partnerships
- **Revenue:** £13.1M (2024)
- **Markets:** 9 countries globally

### Value Proposition:
- **For Students:** Unlimited access to academic content at fraction of textbook purchase costs
- **For Institutions:** Eliminate access barriers, improve engagement tracking, built-in accessibility features
- **For Corporate:** Professional development content library for employee upskilling

### Differentiation:
- Accessibility-first design (Open Dyslexic font, Beeline Reader built-in)
- Cross-disciplinary content access (vs. single textbooks)
- Student engagement analytics for institutions
- Publisher relationships across academic disciplines

1. **Regional Universities** (5,000-50,000 students)
   - Expanding online programs
   - Cost-conscious student populations
   - Need for affordable textbook alternatives
   - Growing diversity and accessibility requirements

2. **Business Schools with Executive Education**
   - Expanding exec ed programs
   - Working professional focus
   - Need for diverse business content
   - Employer-sponsored programs

3. **Online Universities/Programs**
   - 100% or majority-online student population
   - Adult learner focus
   - Affordability mission
   - Flexible content access needs

4. **Community Colleges**
   - Expanding online course offerings
   - Budget-constrained students
   - Growing enrollment (5.4% YoY in 2025)
   - Skills-based, non-degree programs

5. **EdTech Platforms Serving Higher Ed**
   - B2B SaaS for universities
   - Content aggregation models
   - Partnership/integration opportunities

#### Firmographics:
- **Student Population:** 3,000-50,000 students (sweet spot: 10,000-30,000)
- **Online %:** 20%+ of students taking online courses (growing to 50%+)
- **Geographic:** US (priority), UK, Europe, international expansion markets
- **Type:** Public universities, private non-profit, online-only institutions
- **Budget:** Mid-market institutions (not Ivy League, not tiny schools)

## Pain Points by Segment

### Higher Education Pain Points:

1. **Textbook Affordability Crisis**
   - Average student spends $1,200-$1,500/year on textbooks
   - Cost barrier prevents students from purchasing required materials
   - Impact on student success and retention

2. **Accessibility Requirements**
   - ADA compliance mandates
   - Diverse student populations need assistive tech
   - Most textbooks lack built-in accessibility features

3. **Student Engagement & Retention**
   - Need data on student engagement with materials
   - Early warning systems for struggling students
   - Pastoral care and intervention triggers

4. **Limited Content Diversity**
   - Single textbooks constrain interdisciplinary teaching
   - Faculty want to assign readings across multiple sources
   - Copyright/permissions barriers for course packs

5. **Online Program Scaling**
   - Digital transformation initiatives need digital-first content
   - Remote students need 24/7 content access
   - Hybrid models require flexible materials

### Corporate L&D Pain Points:

1. **Skills Gap & Upskilling Urgency**
   - 73% of orgs say human capabilities must keep pace with tech
   - Only 9% making progress on this goal
   - Rapid change in required skills (AI, data, etc.)

2. **Content Access & Costs**
   - Expensive per-employee licensing for learning platforms
   - Limited breadth of professional development content
   - Need for business, technical, and soft skills content

3. **Mandatory Training Requirements**
   - CPE (accounting), CLE (legal), CME (healthcare), FINRA (financial services)
   - Compliance and regulatory training
   - Professional certification maintenance

4. **Employee Demand for Learning**
   - Employees spending 50% more time learning YoY
   - 81% want more skills-based training
   - 80% want personalized learning

5. **ROI Measurement**
   - Need to demonstrate L&D impact on business outcomes
   - Skills gap analytics
   - Employee retention through development

## Anti-ICP (Who NOT to Target)

### Higher Education Anti-ICP:
❌ **Elite Private Universities** (Harvard, Yale, Stanford, MIT, etc.)
   - Already have extensive library resources
   - High library budgets
   - Students can afford textbooks
   - Prestige focus over cost savings

❌ **Very Small Colleges** (<1,000 students)
   - Too small for institutional pricing to make sense
   - Limited budget authority
   - Deal complexity not worth deal size

❌ **For-Profit Universities with Poor Reputation**
   - Regulatory scrutiny
   - Student debt concerns
   - Brand risk association

❌ **Community Colleges with No Online Programs**
   - Not aligned with online expansion trend
   - Less urgent need without online students

### Corporate Anti-ICP:
❌ **Companies <500 Employees**
   - Too small for institutional pricing
   - Informal or no L&D function
   - Limited budget

❌ **Companies with No Formal L&D Function**
   - No budget owner
   - No learning culture
   - Long sales cycle with low close rate

❌ **Manufacturing/Industrial with Limited Knowledge Workers**
   - Content library not relevant (need skills/vocational training)
   - Shop floor workers vs. office workers
   - Different learning modality needs

❌ **Startups in High-Growth Mode**
   - L&D not prioritized yet
   - Budget constraints
   - Too early in maturity

❌ **Public Sector/Government** (initially)
   - Long procurement cycles
   - Budget constraints
   - Complex buying process

## Competitive Landscape

### Higher Education Competitors:
- **Library ebook databases:** ProQuest, EBSCO, JSTOR (Perlego partner via ITHAKA)
- **Textbook rental:** Chegg, Amazon Textbook Rental
- **Course materials:** Top Hat, Akademos
- **OER (Open Educational Resources):** OpenStax, various OER libraries

**Perlego Advantages:**
- Unlimited access model (vs. per-book rental)
- Publisher breadth (9,000+ publishers)
- Built-in accessibility features
- Engagement analytics
- Lower cost than library databases

### Corporate L&D Competitors:
- **Learning platforms:** LinkedIn Learning, Coursera for Business, Udemy for Business
- **Content aggregators:** Skillsoft, Pluralsight, O'Reilly Learning
- **Professional development:** Association of International Certified Professional Accountants (CPE), various CME providers

**Perlego Advantages:**
- Academic publisher content (vs. video courses)
- Professional certification content depth
- Lower cost than specialized platforms
- Cross-disciplinary content access
