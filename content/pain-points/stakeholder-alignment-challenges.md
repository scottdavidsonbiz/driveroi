# Pain Point: Stakeholder Alignment Challenges

## The Pain Point
Companies with multiple decision-makers (partners, co-founders, leadership teams) face slower decision cycles due to need for consensus, alignment, and unanimous buy-in.

---

## How It Manifests

### IVS Tax Services Example

**Decision Structure:**
- **3 partners:** Jeffrey Cohen, Jennifer Cohen, James
- **Unanimous agreement needed:** All three must align on investment
- **Geographic dispersion:** Need in-person meeting to discuss (Fort Worth trip)

**Jeffrey's Quote:**
> "The three of us are going to be together in Fort Worth Sunday, Monday, Tuesday. So I'm hoping... we won't get inundated with people coming into our different offices asking us questions. So that's three of us can actually sit down and talk about this... and I like to make a decision... hopefully by the end of the week."

**Translation:**
- Can't decide alone (even though Jeffrey is champion)
- Need dedicated time to discuss as leadership team
- Decision timeline = when partners can meet + align

---

## Why This Matters (Buying Psychology)

### Extends Sales Cycle
- **Solo decision-maker:** Can decide same day/week
- **Multi-stakeholder:** Need meeting, discussion, alignment = 1-3 weeks minimum
- **IVS example:** Proposal presented 11/3, decision by 11/11+ (1+ week delay)

### Creates Internal Selling Burden
- **Champion (Jeffrey):** Must convince partners (Jennifer, James)
- **Internal sales job:** Explain value, address concerns, get buy-in
- **Your job:** Equip champion with materials to sell internally

### Reveals Stakeholder Dynamics
- **Jeffrey:** Champion, engaged, primary contact
- **Jennifer:** Present on kickoff, needs education, supportive
- **James:** Not present, hesitant (LinkedIn spam concerns), needs convincing

**Each stakeholder = different concerns to address**

### Different from Budget Concern
- **NOT:** "We can't afford this"
- **IS:** "We need everyone to agree this is worth it"

---

## What They've Tried (IVS)

### Sequential Meetings (Inefficient)
- Discovery call: Jeffrey only
- Proposal review: Jeffrey only
- Kickoff: Jeffrey + Jennifer (James absent)
- Fort Worth trip: All three partners finally together

**Result:** Extended sales cycle (need all stakeholders present eventually)

### Champion-Led Internal Selling
- Jeffrey brings proposal to Jennifer + James
- Jeffrey explains value, addresses concerns
- **Risk:** Champion may not position optimally (doesn't know objection handling)

---

## Buying Trigger Formula

```
Multiple decision-makers
+ Need for consensus
+ Champion must sell internally
+ Each stakeholder has different concerns
= Extended sales cycle + need for facilitation
```

## How Scott Addressed It (IVS Engagement)

### Discovery Call (10/29)
**Identified stakeholder structure early:**
- "Who else needs to be involved in this decision?"
- Jeffrey mentioned: Jen, James (partners)
- **Action:** Understood decision would take time

### Proposal Presentation (11/3)
**Equipped champion with materials:**
1. **Sent proposal + pricing breakdown:** Jeffrey can share with partners
2. **Offered group call:** "Would it help to get everyone on a call together?"
3. **Set timeline expectation:** "When do you think you'll have a decision?"
4. **Addressed anticipated objections:** Pricing (ROI math), LinkedIn spam (personalization)

**Jeffrey's response:**
> "Jen and I can can get an idea of that and then we can discuss that with James when he gets back and see about moving forward with it."

### Kickoff Meeting (11/11)
**Brought Jennifer into conversation:**
- Both Jeffrey + Jennifer present
- Jennifer saw tools demo, understood approach
- Asked clarifying questions (subscription fees for tools)
- **Result:** 2 of 3 stakeholders now aligned

**Still needed:** James buy-in (Fort Worth trip)

## Discovery Questions That Surface This Pain

1. **"Who else needs to be involved in this decision?"**
   - Listen for: Multiple names, titles, "we decide as a team"

2. **"What's your decision-making process for investments like this?"**
   - Listen for: Consensus, unanimous, leadership team review

3. **"Is there anyone who would veto or block this, even if you're on board?"**
   - Listen for: Partner dynamics, power structure, veto authority

4. **"What concerns do you anticipate from [other stakeholder]?"**
   - Listen for: Different priorities, hesitations, objections

5. **"When can you get everyone together to discuss?"**
   - Listen for: Timeline, availability, urgency level

## Sales Process Playbook (How to Navigate Multi-Stakeholder Deals)

### 1. Identify Stakeholders Early (Discovery)
**Questions:**
- "Who else needs to be involved in this decision?"
- "Who holds the budget for this?"
- "Who would need to approve this?"
- "Who would be affected by this (and might have opinions)?"

### 2. Equip Champion with Materials
**Provide:**
- Written proposal (they can share)
- Pricing breakdown with ROI math
- FAQ document (anticipated objections)
- Case studies / social proof
- Contract for legal review

**Why:**
- Champion will sell internally (whether you like it or not)
- Better to equip them than let them wing it
- Reduces "telephone game" distortion

---

### 3. Offer Group Presentation
**Pitch:**
> "Would it be helpful to get everyone on a call together? I can walk through the proposal and answer questions directly."

**Benefits:**
- Control narrative (no distortion)
- Address each stakeholder's concerns directly
- Build relationship with all decision-makers
- Accelerate alignment

**When they decline (like IVS):**
- Provide materials
- Ask: "What questions/concerns do you anticipate? Let me address those now"
- Follow up after internal discussion

---

### 4. Address Each Stakeholder's Concerns

**Jeffrey (Champion):**
- Concern: Timeline, results, tax season urgency
- Addressed: Fast setup (Thanksgiving), December launch, momentum before year-end

**Jennifer (CFO/Operations):**
- Concern: Budget, process, infrastructure costs
- Addressed: Pricing breakdown, ownership model, ROI math (1 deal = 3.6x ROI)

**James (Partner, Hesitant):**
- Concern: LinkedIn spam perception, professional reputation
- Addressed: Personalization (not spam), volume limits (25/day), review/approval process

**Strategy:** Anticipate and pre-address each stakeholder's concerns (even if not present)

---

### 5. Set Timeline Expectations
**Ask:**
> "What's the realistic timeline for getting everyone aligned?"

**IVS example:**
- Fort Worth trip: Sunday-Tuesday
- Decision: By end of week (Friday)
- **Total:** 1 week from proposal to decision

**Manage expectations:**
- Understand decision won't be immediate
- Don't pressure prematurely
- Follow up at agreed timeline

---

### 6. Follow Up Strategically
**After champion's internal meeting:**
> "Hi Jeffrey, just following up on your Fort Worth discussion. What questions came up? How can I help address any concerns?"

**If decision delayed:**
> "I understand alignment takes time. What's the main blocker right now? Can I help address it?"

**If stakeholder has specific concern:**
> "I heard James is concerned about LinkedIn spam. Can I send him a quick note explaining our personalization approach?"

## Red Flags to Watch For

### Serial Non-Deciders
- "We've been discussing this for 6 months" (can't align)
- "Everyone has a veto" (no path to yes)
- **Action:** Qualify out (won't close)

### Unclear Authority
- "I think I can approve this" (unsure = risky)
- "Probably just need to mention it to [person]" (underestimating)
- **Action:** Dig deeper on decision process

### Too Many Stakeholders
- 5+ people need to agree
- No clear champion
- **Action:** Qualify carefully (may never align)
