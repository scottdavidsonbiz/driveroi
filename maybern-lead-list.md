## Buyer Personas & Decision Makers

### Primary Decision Makers to Target (Priority Order)

#### 1. Chief Operating Officer (COO) - PRIMARY BUYER
**Title Variations:** COO, President & COO, Chief Administrative Officer

**Why They Care:**
- Responsible for operational efficiency and scalability
- Owns fund operations, investor relations, and administrative functions
- Feels pain of manual processes and administrator coordination
- Measured on operational leverage (AUM/employee ratio)

**Pain Points:**
- Coordinating multiple fund administrators across 5+ funds
- Manual waterfall calculations and capital call/distribution processing
- Investor reporting delays and quality control issues
- Scaling operations without proportional headcount growth

**Messaging Angle:** "Operationalize your fund administration to scale from $10B to $20B without doubling your ops team"

---

#### 2. Chief Financial Officer (CFO) - ECONOMIC BUYER
**Title Variations:** CFO, Chief Accounting Officer, VP of Finance

**Why They Care:**
- Owns financial reporting accuracy and compliance
- Budget authority for operational systems
- Accountable for audit readiness and investor transparency
- Needs to close books faster each quarter

**Pain Points:**
- Reliance on Excel for complex waterfall calculations = audit risk
- Fund administrator errors requiring reconciliation
- Late closes (45+ days) for quarterly financials
- Fee calculation errors and LP inquiries

**Messaging Angle:** "Close your quarterly books in 15 days instead of 45 with automated fund accounting"

---

#### 3. Head of Fund Operations / Fund Controller - CHAMPION
**Title Variations:**
- Head of Fund Operations
- Director of Fund Operations
- Fund Controller
- VP of Fund Accounting & Administration

**Why They Care:**
- Day-to-day owner of fund administration processes
- Most acute pain from manual Excel processes
- Drowning in capital calls, distributions, and waterfall calculations
- First to advocate for better tools

**Pain Points:**
- Manual data entry across multiple fund admin portals
- Reconciling GP and administrator data every quarter
- Training new hires on complex Excel models
- Fire drills every quarter-end and year-end

**Messaging Angle:** "Eliminate the Excel fire drills and give your team time back for strategic work"

---

#### 4. Managing Partner / Founding Partner - INFLUENCER
**Title Variations:** Managing Partner, Founding Partner, Senior Partner, CEO

**Why They Care:**
- Cares about firm reputation and LP satisfaction
- Approves major operational investments
- Feels pressure when LPs complain about reporting delays
- Wants to professionalize operations as firm scales

**Pain Points:**
- LP complaints about reporting delays or errors
- Difficulty raising next fund due to operational reputation
- Inability to launch new strategies due to ops constraints
- Embarrassment when administrator makes mistakes

**Messaging Angle:** "Institutional-grade operations that help you raise your next fund faster"

---

#### 5. Investor Relations / Head of LP Relations - INFLUENCER
**Title Variations:** Head of Investor Relations, VP of Investor Relations, Director of LP Relations

**Why They Care:**
- Front-line with LP complaints about reporting
- Needs accurate data to answer LP questions quickly
- Wants to look professional and responsive to LPs

**Pain Points:**
- Can't answer LP questions without waiting for administrator
- Reporting delays damage LP relationships
- Complex questions require diving into Excel models
- Limited visibility into real-time fund performance

**Messaging Angle:** "Give LPs the transparency and responsiveness they expect from a $10B+ manager"

---
