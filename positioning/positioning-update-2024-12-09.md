# Positioning Update: December 9, 2024
**Based on:** 7 conversations across 5 distinct companies (Oct 29 - Nov 24, 2025)
**Note:** Additional conversation with Treasury Hub (SaaS treasury management for W. Africa/LatAm) not included due to lack of transcript
**Analyzed:**
- 2025-10-29: Jeff (IVS Tax Services) - Discovery [NON-SAAS]
- 2025-10-31: Ben (PatientFi) - Clay project scoping [SAAS - Fintech]
- 2025-11-03: Jeff (IVS Tax) - Proposal review [NON-SAAS]
- 2025-11-11: Miguel (IVS Home Health) - Sales planning [NON-SAAS]
- 2025-11-14: Jeffrey & Jennifer (IVS Tax) - Kickoff [NON-SAAS]
- 2025-11-24: Keegan (Voice Brain) - Discovery [SAAS - Public Safety]
- 2025-11-24: Gautier (Environmental data startup) - Discovery [SAAS - Data Platform]

## Executive Summary
**Caveat: Limited data (7-8 conversations) - these are hypotheses to test, not definitive conclusions.**

**Mix breakdown:** 4 SaaS companies (PatientFi, Voice Brain, Gautier's startup, Treasury Hub) vs 3 non-SaaS (IVS Tax, IVS Home Health).

**Key finding:** The strongest prospect (IVS Tax - 3 calls, reached kickoff stage) is a founder-led legacy business with outdated GTM processes. However, this could indicate two distinct opportunities rather than "avoid SaaS":

**Hypothesis 1: Founder-led legacy businesses** (like IVS Tax/Home Health)
- Established (5+ years), 15-50 employees
- Founders/partners are decision-makers with budget authority
- Manual/outdated GTM processes desperately need modernization
- Can't DIY like tech-savvy founders

**Hypothesis 2: SaaS companies in finance/accounting vertical** (like Treasury Hub)
- Leverage your FP&A background from 6sense
- RevOps + finance expertise is rare combination
- You speak their language (metrics, financial planning, revenue operations)
- These companies need someone who understands both GTM AND financial operations

**To avoid:** Individual contributors without budget authority (Ben's $500 offer), regardless of company type or industry.

## Pain Point Patterns

### High Frequency (appeared in 6-7 conversations)

1. **Manual, inefficient GTM processes**
   - Appeared in: ALL 7 conversations
   - Segments: Both SaaS and non-SaaS, all company sizes
   - Examples:
     - Jeff: "Current marketer making excuses, cold calls/email blasts not working"
     - Ben: "Manual LinkedIn research, using free Hunter tool, archaic process"
     - Keegan: "Manually calling police chiefs, can't scale current approach"
     - Gautier: "Pipeline generation, ICP outreach, data enrichment are blurry to me"
   - Our solution: Modern automation infrastructure (Clay, sequencing, intent data) + strategic guidance

2. **Lack of modern infrastructure**
   - Appeared in: 6 conversations (Jeff, Ben, Miguel, Keegan, Gautier)
   - Segments: Primarily non-SaaS and early-stage companies
   - Examples:
     - Jeff: Using Google Sheets as CRM
     - Ben: "Messy CRM (Sugar), incorrect/incomplete data"
     - Keegan: "Lapsed Zoom Info, no sequencing until Clarify releases theirs"
     - Gautier: "Just signed HubSpot last week, need to set up"
   - Our solution: Set up proper tech stack from scratch, infrastructure ownership model

3. **Resource constraints / bandwidth crunch**
   - Appeared in: 5 conversations (Ben, Miguel, Keegan, Gautier, Jeff indirectly)
   - Segments: Across all types, but especially solo operators and small teams
   - Examples:
     - Keegan: "Small team, Keegan wearing all hats (SDR/AE/AM)"
     - Gautier: "Team of one, limited knowledge on certain aspects"
     - Miguel: "Team lacks bandwidth to connect the dots between market data and sales territories"
     - Ben: "Trying to prove ROI with limited personal/company resources"
   - Our solution: Consultative approach that doesn't require hand-holding + automation to save time

### Medium Frequency (appeared in 3-4 conversations)

4. **Strategic uncertainty / lack of clarity on ICP**
   - Appeared in: 4 conversations (Jeff, Miguel, Keegan, Gautier)
   - Examples:
     - Jeff: "No systematic approach to ICP definition, segmentation"
     - Keegan: "Need to decide between federal focus vs. state/local"
     - Gautier: "Still in discovery mode, forming idea of support model"
   - Our solution: ICP definition workshops, data-driven refinement through outreach testing

5. **Current solution actively failing**
   - Appeared in: 3 conversations (Jeff, Ben, Keegan)
   - Examples:
     - Jeff: "Competitors growing while IVS plateaus, current marketer making excuses"
     - Ben: "Acknowledges current process is inefficient"
     - Keegan: "Missing RFP opportunities, finding contracts after due dates"
   - Our solution: Replace failing approach with modern, automated system

### Segment-Specific Pain Points

**Non-SaaS Companies (IVS Tax, IVS Home Health, PatientFi):**
- Outdated, manual processes ("Google Sheets as CRM")
- Legacy industries resisting modernization
- Stagnant growth despite years in business
- Don't know where to start with modern GTM

**Early-Stage Companies (Voice Brain, Gautier's startup):**
- Strategic uncertainty (market focus, ICP definition)
- Need to set up infrastructure from scratch
- Solo operators wearing too many hats
- Need to prove value before scaling investment

**Individual Contributors (Ben):**
- Limited budget authority
- Need quick wins to justify spend
- Pressure to perform with inadequate tools
- Less decision-making power

## Market Segmentation

### Segment 1: Established Non-SaaS Services Businesses (HIGHEST OPPORTUNITY)
- **Profile:** 15-50 employees, 5-25 years in business, legacy industries (professional services, healthcare, fintech services)
- **Sample size:** 3 conversations (IVS Tax, IVS Home Health, PatientFi aspects)
- **Key pain points:**
  - Manual/outdated GTM processes (all)
  - Stagnant growth despite market opportunity (all)
  - Legacy tools or no proper CRM (all)
  - Resource constraints despite established business (all)
- **Buying triggers:**
  - Seasonal/planning cycles creating urgency
  - Growth goals not being met
  - Current solution actively failing
  - Competitive pressure
- **Objections:**
  - Partner buy-in needed
  - Concern about time investment to explain industry
  - Want someone who understands business, not just tech
- **Value props that resonate:**
  - Consultative + technical (not just "tell me what to build")
  - Modern automation vs. manual legacy processes
  - Infrastructure ownership (avoid vendor lock-in)
  - Business acumen ("understands SaaS metrics and scaling mechanics")
- **Assessment:** **STRONGEST FIT.** These companies have budget, urgency, and desperately need what you offer. They're not competing with 1000 RevOps consultants targeting SaaS. Jeff moved through 3 conversations toward kickoff.

### Segment 2: Early-Stage Startups (5-20 employees, scaling)
- **Profile:** Pre-revenue to early revenue, 5-20 employees, rapid growth plans, tech or data companies
- **Sample size:** 2 conversations (Voice Brain, Gautier's startup)
- **Key pain points:**
  - Strategic uncertainty (which market to target)
  - Solo operator with knowledge gaps
  - Need infrastructure from scratch
  - Resource constraints
- **Buying triggers:**
  - Major validation event (funding, big customer win, CRADA)
  - Hiring plans (need infrastructure before reps start)
  - Q1/Q2 planning cycles
  - Just implemented CRM
- **Objections:**
  - Company size ("smaller than your target segment")
  - Timeline (Q1, not immediate)
  - Need to secure buy-in from CEO/partners
- **Value props that resonate:**
  - Consultative approach
  - Set up infrastructure correctly from day one
  - Save SDR/rep time through automation
- **Assessment:** **MODERATE FIT.** They need you but have budget constraints and longer decision cycles. Gautier explicitly said "smaller than target segment" but still wanted to talk. These deals take longer to close.

### Segment 3: Individual GTM Operators at Larger Companies
- **Profile:** SDR/AE/GTM manager at established company, limited budget authority, need to prove ROI
- **Sample size:** 1 conversation (Ben at PatientFi)
- **Key pain points:**
  - Manual work consuming time
  - Messy data
  - Need to prove value before getting company buy-in
- **Buying triggers:**
  - Pressure to perform
  - Need quick wins
  - Budget became available (even if small)
- **Objections:**
  - **MAJOR:** Budget constraints ($500 vs. market rate)
  - Need to prove ROI before spending more
  - Less decision-making authority
- **Value props that resonate:**
  - Quick wins to prove value
  - Phased approach (small scope first)
- **Assessment:** **LOWEST FIT.** Ben offered $500 for work worth $3K+. These prospects lack budget authority and decision-making power. They need to "prove ROI" before committing, which is a red flag for scope creep and undervaluation.

### SaaS vs Non-SaaS Analysis (CORRECTED)

**SaaS Segment:**
- Conversations: 4 (Voice Brain, PatientFi, Gautier's data startup, Treasury Hub)
- Breakdown:
  - **Fintech/Finance:** PatientFi (BNPL), Treasury Hub (treasury management) - 2 companies
  - **Data/AI:** Voice Brain (public safety), Gautier's startup (environmental data) - 2 companies
- Common characteristics: Mix of early-stage (Voice Brain, Gautier) and more established (PatientFi has enterprise sales)
- Saturation assessment: **NUANCED.** General SaaS may be saturated, but there could be sub-segments (e.g., finance/accounting SaaS) where your FP&A background creates differentiation.
- Opportunity: **HYPOTHESIS TO TEST:** Target SaaS companies in finance/accounting vertical where RevOps + FP&A expertise is rare and valuable.

**Non-SaaS Segment:**
- Conversations: 3 (IVS Tax, IVS Home Health) - both founder-led legacy businesses
- Common characteristics:
  - Legacy industries with outdated processes
  - 10-25 years in business but no modern GTM
  - Don't understand modern tools (Jeff using Google Sheets, Miguel can't synthesize data)
  - Willing to pay for expertise because they can't DIY
  - Established revenue but stagnant growth
  - Founders/partners are decision-makers with budget authority
- Opportunity: **HIGH - VALIDATED BY IVS TAX PROGRESS.**
  - IVS Tax moved fastest through pipeline (3 calls, reached kickoff)

  - These companies desperately need modernization

**Key Insight:** It may be less about "SaaS vs non-SaaS" and more about:
1. **Decision-maker access** (founders/partners vs individual contributors)
2. **Budget authority** (established budget vs "need to prove ROI")
3. **Stage** (established 5+ years vs early-stage)
4. **Your unique expertise fit** (finance/accounting SaaS, founder-led legacy businesses)
